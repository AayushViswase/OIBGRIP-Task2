A time line

Edson Arantes do Nascimento

17-year-old Pele received unanimous recognition from experts, spectators and rivals and became the youngest champion in the history of world championships.

Pelé began playing for Santos at age 15 and the Brazil national football team at 16. During his international career, he won three FIFA World Cups : 1958 , 1962 and 1970 , being the only player ever to do so.

Here's a time line of Pelé

  • Born 23 October 1940, Três Corações, Minas Gerais, Brazil
  • 1956 - he signed a professional contract with the club in "Santos".
  • 1957 - in this season season started, Pelé was given a starting place in the first team and, at the age of 16, became the top scorer in the league.
  • 1957 - Ten months after signing professionally, the teenager was called up to the Brazil national team.
  • 1958 - Pelé won his first FIFA World Cups. He was the youngest player of that tournament, and at the time the youngest ever to play in the World Cup.
  • 1962 - Won again FIFA World Cups.
  • 1970 - The final tournament of 1970 (the fourth in the sports biography of Pele) became a triumphal one - for him personally, and for the whole team, whose composition in this championship many experts are the strongest in the history of the Brazilian national team.
  • 1975 - Finished performances for Santos and the national team.
  • 1975 - Pele signed a contract with the American professional club "Cosmos".
  • October 1, 1977 Pele, who added to his extensive collection, who took the title of US champion, played a farewell match in which he played for Cosmos and Santos at one time, and completed his football career.
  • 1995 - Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso appointed Pelé to the position of Extraordinary Minister for Sport, and he was also appointed a UNESCO Goodwill Ambassador.
  • 1999 - The IOC called Pele the greatest athlete of the century (although he never participated in the Olympics)
Pele always remained an adherent of the team game.

"Without a common effort, a victory in football is impossible," he says. "Football is a team, a team, not one or two or three star players."
